Canada Association of Security and Intelligence Studies Vancouver

CASIS Vancouver develops best practices for the application of intelligence in policing, military, and public safety spaces. We drive the evolution of intelligence practice and adapt alongside the ever-evolving threat landscape. By keeping pace with the contemporary threat environment, we uphold safety and security and assist in the preservation of democracy. In particular, we ensure that intelligence practice, intelligence tradecraft, intelligence accountability, intelligence methodology, and intelligence products are best suited to allow the police, military, and public safety sectors to contend with the development of violence, transnational threat, and emerging security issues.

About Us

Established in 2018, the Canadian Association for Security and Intelligence Studies Vancouver (CASIS Vancouver) functions as the West Coast chapter of the Canadian Association for Security and Intelligence Studies. Its purpose is to provide informed debate on security and intelligence issues on the West Coast of Canada.

West Coast Security Conference

The Canadian Association for Security and Intelligence Studies Vancouver hosts the annual CASIS West Coast Security Conference every November.

Its objective is to encourage engagement on issues pertaining to the dialogue on security, extremism, and public safety on the West Coast. The conference features specialists in the fields of intelligence, hybrid warfare, contemporary conflict, identity conflict, and fifth-generation warfare.

The West Coast Security Conference features experts and practitioners with whom participants will be able to interact with. Topics include:

  • Intelligence Practice
  • The Evolution of the Intelligence Cycle
  • The Application of Intelligence to Contemporary Threat Problems
  • The Contemporary Threat Environment
  • Proactive Policing

The conference features two days of online interactive speaker panels with leading experts and practitioners which will provide participants with an opportunity to expand their professional knowledge and network, while engaging in discussions of the most critical contemporary security problems. It also features two days of interactive competency-based workshops.

What we do

Research and Publications

The Canadian Association for Security and Intelligence Studies Vancouver conducts research on soft violence, Violent Transnational Social Movements, and contemporary conflict through its research lab, the Canadian Centre for Identity-Based Conflict. Publications from the CCIBC are released through our website and through the Journal of Intelligence, Conflict, and Warfare (JICW).

Instructional Teams

The Canadian Association for Security and Intelligence Studies Vancouver provides certified instructional teams to conduct seminars and table-top exercises. Our instructional teams specialize in offering customized training in a variety of analytical and critical thinking-based topics as well as academic-based trend & pattern analysis of contemporary conflict developments.

Roundtable Briefing Notes

Each roundtable generates a Briefing Note which include key points and key takeaways. There is a focus group and survey conducted for each event. These are designed in consultation with each speaker. These resources are available to CASIS Vancouver members for research purposes. Members are encouraged to submit questions which might inform their ongoing research or assist with security problem-solving initiatives.

Analytical Training Courses

The Canadian Association for Security and Intelligence Studies Vancouver is offering training programs that will allow participants to gain a deeper understanding of Structured Analytical Techniques. Using in-house developed textbooks, our training program incorporates both diagnostic and contrarian techniques and is taught by our own analysts and researchers.

Journal of Intelligence, Conflict, and Warfare

The Journal of Intelligence, Conflict, and Warfare (JICW) is a bi-annual, peer-reviewed journal published by the Canadian Association for Security and Intelligence Studies Vancouver and Simon Fraser University Library. This journal is distributed to a variety of institutions and each issue provides readers with perspectives from law enforcement practitioners, policy-makers, military officials, and academic researchers on issues in multiple disciplines related to intelligence and security.

Defence and Security Advisory Network (DSAN)

DSAN brings together experts in defence, security, intelligence, diplomacy from multiple organizations. This network is a hub for dialogue, publishing papers, providing a point of contact for one another to coordinate, collaborate on research, and exchange ideas on the nature of security threats and response re: Canada, allies, international system. The aim of DSAN is to provide a home for Pacific and West Coast-based security and defence scholars. The network establishes a defence and security research hub which maintains a research interests list, biographies, and papers – both research and opinion pieces.

Structured Analytical Textbooks

Critical thinking is the most important tool available to an analyst. The ability to be objective when assessing information requires both art and scientific application. The purpose of this volume is to assist new analysts and researchers with a series of table-top exercises to assist with the drilling and development of analytical expertise.

Theoretical Models

We develop theoretical models, such as: Pinehurst Model, Counter Violence Grid,Discord Model, Heat Map Violent Transnational Social Movements (VTSMs), Soft Violence in the context of VTSMs.

Online Courses

CASIS Vancouver provides a variety of internal and external training through the use of Moodle, a Learning Management System. These courses are delivered by instructors certified in Military Methods of Instruction and cover a range of intelligence and national security topics. For further information, please contact info@casisvancouver.ca.
